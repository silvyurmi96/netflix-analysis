Overview

What this project is about

I downloaded the Netflix Movies and TV Shows dataset from Kaggle and ran an exploratory analysis using Python and SQL. The goal was simple — poke around the data and see what patterns show up. What kind of content does Netflix have? Where does it come from? Who is it aimed at?

The dataset covers 8,807 titles with information on type, country, director, cast, rating, genre and release year. Around 30% of titles had no director listed, which was one of the more unexpected things I found.

🎬
Netflix is primarily a movie platform. With 6,131 movies vs 2,676 TV shows, movies make up 70% of the library. That ratio is probably not what most people would guess.
🌍
The US dominates by a wide margin. With 2,818 titles, the US produces nearly 3 times more Netflix content than India in second place at 972. After that the numbers drop off sharply.
🔞
Netflix targets adults. TV-MA is the most common rating at 3,207 titles (36%), followed by TV-14 at 2,160 (25%). Together they account for 61% of all content.
⏱️
The average Netflix movie is exactly 100 minutes long. Calculated after cleaning out 3 rows where duration data was missing.
🎭
Dramas and Documentaries lead the genres. The most common genre combination is Dramas and International Movies at 362 titles, followed closely by Documentaries at 359 and Stand-Up Comedy at 334.
